# Document Control Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes a consistent method for creating, reviewing, approving, updating, storing, and retiring controlled documents.

## Scope

This policy applies to governance, risk, compliance, security, operations, safety, audit, and incident documentation.

## Controlled Documents

Controlled documents may include:

- Policies
- Procedures
- Standards
- Templates
- Risk registers
- Evidence registers
- Incident forms
- Audit checklists
- Training records
- Executive reports

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Document Owner | Maintains accuracy, relevance, and review schedule. |
| Reviewer | Reviews content for accuracy, completeness, and business need. |
| Approver | Confirms the document is approved for use. |
| User | Uses the current approved version and reports needed updates. |

## Version Control Standard

Each controlled document should include:

- Document title
- Owner
- Version number
- Effective date
- Last review date
- Next review date
- Change summary
- Approval status

## Review Cadence

Controlled documents should be reviewed at least annually or when major changes occur, such as process updates, regulatory changes, incidents, audit findings, or organizational changes.

## Change Control

Changes should be documented in a change log. Major changes should be reviewed and approved before publication.

## Storage Standard

Current approved documents should be stored in a known location. Drafts, archived versions, and retired versions should be clearly labeled to prevent accidental use.

## Policy Principle

Documentation is not only administrative. It is a control that supports accountability, continuity, audit readiness, and consistent execution.
