# Governance Charter

## Purpose

This charter defines the purpose, scope, ownership, and operating principles for a lightweight governance, risk, compliance, and documentation program.

## Program Mission

The mission of this governance program is to create structure, accountability, documentation quality, risk visibility, and audit readiness across business operations.

## Scope

This framework applies to:

- Governance documentation
- Risk tracking
- Evidence management
- Document control
- Incident documentation
- Audit readiness
- Change tracking
- Issue resolution
- Executive reporting

## Program Objectives

1. Identify and track operational, compliance, documentation, and security risks.
2. Maintain current and traceable governance documents.
3. Preserve evidence needed for audits, investigations, reviews, and management decisions.
4. Establish clear ownership for risks, controls, documents, and issues.
5. Support consistent reporting to leadership.
6. Improve accountability through documented decisions and change history.

## Governance Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Executive Sponsor | Provides oversight and confirms business priorities. |
| Governance Owner | Maintains the governance framework and reporting cadence. |
| Risk Owner | Tracks assigned risks and treatment actions. |
| Document Owner | Maintains controlled documents and review schedules. |
| Evidence Owner | Ensures supporting evidence is current and accessible. |
| Control Owner | Maintains assigned control activities and remediation tasks. |

## Operating Principles

- Evidence before claims
- Ownership before escalation
- Current documents over outdated files
- Repeatable process over informal memory
- Clear decisions over ambiguous assumptions
- Continuous improvement over one-time cleanup

## Review Cadence

The governance framework should be reviewed quarterly, or when there are major changes to business operations, regulatory requirements, audit findings, incidents, or leadership priorities.

## Success Measures

- Risks are identified, owned, and reviewed.
- Evidence is organized and traceable.
- Controlled documents are current and approved.
- Issues are tracked to closure.
- Major decisions are documented.
- Leadership has visibility into risk and compliance status.

## Charter Principle

Governance is not bureaucracy. It is the structure that helps an organization make better decisions, reduce preventable risk, preserve evidence, and operate with accountability.
