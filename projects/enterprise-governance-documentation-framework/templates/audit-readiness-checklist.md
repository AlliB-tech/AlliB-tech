# Audit Readiness Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist helps an organization prepare for internal reviews, compliance checks, security assessments, vendor reviews, or formal audits by confirming that key governance evidence is current, organized, and traceable.

## Audit Preparation Areas

| Area | Question | Evidence Needed | Owner | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Governance | Is there a current governance charter or program overview? | Governance charter | Compliance / Operations | Open | Confirms program scope and accountability. |
| Document Control | Are controlled documents versioned, owned, reviewed, and approved? | Document control policy, version log | Document Owner | Open | Confirms current documents are being managed. |
| Risk Management | Is there a current risk register? | Risk register, risk review notes | Risk Owner | Open | Confirms risks are identified and reviewed. |
| Evidence Management | Is audit evidence stored in a known location? | Evidence register | Compliance / Operations | Open | Confirms traceability. |
| Incident Response | Is there a documented process for reporting and tracking incidents? | Incident report template, escalation workflow | Security / Operations | Open | Confirms response readiness. |
| Training | Are required training records maintained? | Training tracker, completion records | HR / Compliance | Open | Confirms user awareness. |
| Change Control | Are major process or document changes recorded? | Change log, approval records | Document Owner | Open | Confirms controlled updates. |
| Corrective Actions | Are issues tracked through closure? | Issue log, action tracker | Control Owner | Open | Confirms remediation accountability. |
| Executive Reporting | Are major risks and open issues summarized for leadership? | Executive summary, dashboard | Compliance / Operations | Open | Confirms management visibility. |

## Audit Readiness Standard

A system is audit-ready when evidence is current, owned, complete, organized, and linked to a business process, risk, control, requirement, or decision.

## Notes

This checklist is intentionally lightweight. It can be adapted for cybersecurity, construction safety, operations, vendor risk, document control, and compliance workflows.
