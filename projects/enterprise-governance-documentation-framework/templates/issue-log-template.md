# Issue Log Template

## Purpose

An issue log tracks problems, gaps, delays, control weaknesses, documentation failures, audit findings, operational blockers, and corrective actions until they are resolved.

## Issue Log

| Issue ID | Date Opened | Issue Description | Category | Impact | Priority | Owner | Root Cause / Suspected Cause | Corrective Action | Due Date | Status | Closure Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I-001 | TBD | Controlled documents do not have consistent version numbers. | Document Control | Medium | High | Document Owner | No formal naming/version standard | Publish document control policy and naming convention | TBD | Open | Updated document inventory |
| I-002 | TBD | Evidence is stored across multiple locations without a central tracker. | Evidence Management | High | High | Compliance Owner | No evidence register | Create evidence register and assign evidence owners | TBD | Open | Completed evidence register |
| I-003 | TBD | Incident follow-up actions are not consistently tracked. | Incident Response | High | Medium | Security / Operations | No lessons learned or action tracker | Add incident report and lessons learned template | TBD | Open | Closed action items |

## Priority Guide

- **High:** Could affect safety, compliance, audit readiness, legal defensibility, security, customer trust, or business continuity.
- **Medium:** Could delay work, create confusion, reduce quality, or increase rework.
- **Low:** Improvement item with limited immediate risk.

## Closure Standard

An issue should not be closed until the corrective action is completed and evidence of completion is stored or linked.

## Principle

Issues become risks when they are ignored. A strong issue log turns problems into accountable action.
